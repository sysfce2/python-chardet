# Equivalences split into evaluation and output_names

`chardet.equivalences` historically held both test-time correctness predicates (is a detected encoding/language acceptable given an expected one?) and runtime output transforms (remap detected codecs to chardet's display names, or to Windows supersets when `prefer_superset=True`). These look related — both encode encoding-equivalence facts — but they operate at completely different times on different units. `is_correct` is test-only and accepts directional supersets and grouped variants; `apply_compat_names` and `apply_preferred_superset` mutate the dict returned to callers and never see a "what was expected" signal. Conflating them invited bugs where a test-acceptance rule could silently leak into runtime output, or a public-API codename remap could leak into accuracy scoring.

In 7.5.0 the module was split: `chardet.evaluation` owns the test-time predicates (`is_correct`, `is_equivalent_detection`, `is_language_equivalent`, plus the encoding/language equivalence groups); `chardet.output_names` owns the runtime transforms (`apply_compat_names`, `apply_preferred_superset`, plus the legacy-name and superset-preference maps). `chardet.equivalences` remains as a deprecation shim that re-exports both surfaces, scheduled for removal in 8.0.

Resist re-merging these "since they're both about encoding equivalences." The underlying encoding-relationship facts are shared, but the *application* of those facts isn't — re-merging would re-introduce the leakage risk and obscure which surface a given symbol participates in.
